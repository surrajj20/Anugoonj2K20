Classical Dance

Classical Dance

Shastriya Nritiya or Indian Classical dance is well known all over the world. Not only is Classical Dance an integral part of the rich tapestry of Indian Culture but it’s also an excellent medium of expression for depicting mythological stories and tales as old as time.

If you possess the grace, poise and skill for Classical Dance then sign up for one of the most elegant competitions organised at Anugoonj.

Rules

  1. One participant and Three accompanists allowed.
  2. Time Limit: 6 + 4 minutes.
  3. Judgment will be based on rhythm, formation, synchronization, expressions, costumes, make up, sets, and overall effects.
  4. No bollywood song allowed.
  5. Restriction to one particular classical dance form.
  6. Negative marks for exceeding time limit.
Date : 18th February 2020 Time : 11:00 am
Location : D-Block Auditorium

Rangoli Making

Rangoli Making

"Rangoli is a colorful art that enhances the beauty of an occasion."

Creating Rangoli is an art. It is considered as an art form which is widely used in and originated in Indian subcontinent. It is an art in which patterns are created on the floor or the ground. In order to create a Rangoli many colors and colorized substance are used such as colored rice, dry flour, colored sand or flower petals. It is usually made during Diwali. But Rangoli is not limited to Diwali festival people create Rangoli’s on Tihar, Onam, Pongal and other Hindu festivals.

So if you’ve got the talent and the vision to create and an innate passion for art, then sign up for the Rangoli Making event this Anugoonj.

Rules

  1. Maximum of two participants per team from each zone will be selected.
  2. No type of paints (poster, oil or water) to be used.
  3. No props (including cone, sieve etc) to be used
  4. The topic will be provided on the spot.
  5. Time Limit: 2+½ hours.
  6. Use of food material leads to wastage offood. Participants should avoid using food grains.
  7. Maximum size of rangloi should be 4x4 feet.
